I'm Alex. I make and write about new technology and games. I also make holograms at Looking Glass.

Game/Art Projects

Embedded: AI Search Redesigned

Redesigned AI search tying every part of an AI-generated answer to a real, visible source. A design experiment to make AI more transparent, giving credit back to content creators. Try it here.

A web game depicting an environment in different representational modes. Play it here.

Space Kitty

A digital and physical game where players use the flashlight from their phones to protect a space kitty from dogliens. Collaboration with Karina Popp. Play it here.

Small Song

A short experimental interactive interpretation of A.R. Ammons's twelve word poem, Small Song. It uses the player's real-world location and time to determine the sun position and lighting. Play it here.

Selected Professional Work

Liteforms

Liteforms is a desktop application that lets users talk to and create holographic characters. It's a design exploration of embodied AI, made for Looking Glass displays. I'm the product lead. Find out more here.

Holographic Elemental

For the release of Pixar's elemental, they ported the two protagonists, Ember and Wade, to the Looking Glass. I supported their team with visual design for the Looking Glass device. Read more about the project here.

Bulgari Holographic Experience

In 2023, Bulgari held a pop-up holographic experience in Rome showcasing one of the world's thinnest watches, the Octo Finissimo Ultra. I worked one the interaction and visual design. See a video of the installation here.

Investing in Futures

A card game that prompts players to imagine alternative global futures. I contributed to the game design of this project by More&More Unlimited.

Holo Weather

An app for Looking Glass Factory's HoloPlayer One that allows the user to see the weather in cities around the world through voice commands and the Amazon Echo. Art by Jeff Chang.

DESIGNxBOULDER

An interactive visualization of projects by artist Natalie Jeremijenko, displayed at the Boulder Museum of Contemporary Art from June to September 2016. See more here.
